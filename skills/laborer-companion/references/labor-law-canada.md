# Canada Employment Law Map

> **Last verified**: 2026-04-29
>
> Not legal advice. Canada is province-specific, and common-law reasonable notice often matters more than minimum employment standards.

## Core Framework

- Federal jurisdiction covers banks, telecom, interprovincial transportation and other federally regulated employers.
- Most employees are covered by provincial employment standards: Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec, etc.
- Human rights law protects against discrimination and accommodation failures.
- Common-law wrongful dismissal can exceed statutory minimums unless a valid contract limits entitlements.

## Key Rules

### Termination and severance

- Employers can often terminate without cause, but they usually owe notice or pay in lieu.
- Statutory minimums are only the floor.
- Common-law reasonable notice considers factors such as age, length of service, character of employment, availability of similar employment, and other facts. Senior/long-tenured employees can have substantial claims.
- "Just cause" is hard for employers to prove in Canada. A PIP does not automatically eliminate notice/severance rights.

### Ontario example

- Ontario distinguishes termination pay from severance pay.
- Ontario severance pay generally requires 5+ years of service plus employer payroll/closure thresholds, and can be up to 26 weeks under ESA rules.
- Common-law notice may still be higher than ESA minimums.

### Working time and overtime

Province-specific. Examples:

- Ontario: overtime often after 44 hours/week.
- Quebec: often after 40 hours/week.
- BC: daily and weekly overtime rules can apply.

Do not answer a Canadian overtime question without province and role.

### PIP

PIPs exist, but are not a magic "cause" builder. Employers still need fair notice of concerns, reasonable goals, support, time to improve, and proportionate response. Many dismissed employees still have notice/severance claims.

## Practical Reality

Canadian employers often offer a low first severance package. The user should not sign immediately. A short consultation with an employment lawyer can materially change outcome.

## Lawyer / Official Help Triggers

- Any dismissal/severance offer, especially 2+ years service or professional/senior role.
- Constructive dismissal: demotion, pay cut, forced resignation, toxic change.
- Human rights issue: disability, pregnancy, family status, age, race, sex/gender, religion.
- Non-compete, non-solicit, IP, equity, commission, bonus dispute.
