# India Labour Law Map

> **Last verified**: 2026-04-29
>
> Not legal advice. India has central labour codes, state rules, industry practice, and major worker-classification differences. IT/services employees often sit in a difficult grey zone.

## Core Framework

India consolidated many labour laws into four labour codes:

- Code on Wages, 2019
- Industrial Relations Code, 2020
- Code on Social Security, 2020
- Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020

The Government of India announced implementation of the four codes effective 2025-11-21. Draft/central/state rules and practical enforcement may still be developing, so always check official Ministry updates and state rules.

## Key Rules And Risk Areas

### Worker classification

The first question is whether the user is treated as a "worker/workman" or as managerial/supervisory/non-workman. White-collar and IT employees may have weaker practical protection if classified outside workman protections.

### Hours and overtime

General labour-code framing includes daily/weekly limits and overtime premium concepts, but sector, state, establishment type, and classification matter. Do not give a precise overtime answer without state, role, and classification.

### Termination / retrenchment

- Retrenchment, layoff, closure and standing-order obligations vary by establishment size, sector and classification.
- The Industrial Relations Code changes thresholds and procedures compared with older law.
- Many IT/services companies use "forced resignation" to avoid formal termination/retrenchment process.

### IT / services practice

Common issues:

- Forced resignation under pressure.
- Bench status used as precursor to exit.
- Bond/training repayment clauses.
- Notice-period buyout disputes.
- Wage arrears, variable pay, final settlement delays.

Do not tell the user to resign. If pressured, advise documenting the pressure, asking for written reasons, and consulting a labour lawyer before signing.

## AI / Monitoring Issues

India's new labour-code framework does not give a simple AI-displacement rule. Issues usually arise through termination, restructuring, data/privacy, performance scoring, and contract obligations.

## Lawyer / Official Help Triggers

- Forced resignation, termination, retrenchment, layoff, or bench-to-exit pressure.
- Bond/training repayment, notice-period penalty, non-compete/non-solicit.
- Wage arrears, final settlement, unpaid variable pay, PF/gratuity dispute.
- Harassment, discrimination, maternity, disability, whistleblowing/retaliation.
