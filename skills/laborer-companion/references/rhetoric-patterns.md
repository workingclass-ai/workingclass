# 职场话术模式库 / Workplace Rhetoric Patterns

This is a reference library for the `/decode` command. It contains 18 documented manipulation patterns organized into 6 categories. When analyzing user-provided workplace text, match each sentence against these patterns systematically.

---

## Category 1: 虚拟亲属化话术 / Pseudo-Family Rhetoric

Core mechanism: blur the line between employment and family relationship, demanding family-level commitment while offering only contractual treatment.

### Pattern 1: "我们是一个家庭" / "We are a family"

- **Real intent**: Get the user to accept unpaid overtime, holiday work, working while sick. Family doesn't track these.
- **Historical origin**: Post-WWII Japanese "corporate familism." Originally paired with lifetime employment. Today's Western companies want the rhetoric without the corresponding commitment.
- **Recommended response**: Insist on contractual relationship standards. Family wouldn't put you on a PIP. Family wouldn't restructure away your role during parental leave.
- **Sample reply**: "I appreciate the warmth, but I'd prefer we build our cooperation on professional grounds: clear responsibilities, fair compensation, defined boundaries."

### Pattern 2: "把公司当成自己的事业" / "Treat the company as your own business"

- **Real intent**: Get founder-level effort while paying employee-level compensation.
- **Historical origin**: Silicon Valley startup culture. Originally paired with real equity. Today most companies want the rhetoric without offering real ownership.
- **Recommended response**: Ask directly: "If I should treat it as mine, how much equity do I get?" If answer is vague, this is rhetoric. If specific, it becomes a real business negotiation.

### Pattern 3: "你不只是来打工的" / "You're not just here to work"

- **Real intent**: Blur the user's identity as a worker so they accept things workers shouldn't accept.
- **Recommended response**: "I'm not just here to work，I'm also here to earn, learn, and grow. But all those goals require basic worker protections. Without those, 'not just working' becomes meaningless."

## Category 2: 模糊未来奖励话术 / Vague Future Reward Rhetoric

Core mechanism: trade real present effort for vague future rewards that never materialize.

### Pattern 4: "我们要相信公司的长期愿景" / "Trust the long-term vision"

- **Real intent**: Get user to accept low present compensation in exchange for hollow "long-term."
- **Key tests**: Does this "vision" have specific timelines? Specific mechanisms (vesting, salary milestones, promotion timeline)? What's the company's historical fulfillment rate on similar promises?
- **Recommended response**: "I believe in the long term too. So let's write it down. In 18 months, if I achieve X, Y, Z, I'm promoted to next level with N% raise. Can we put this in writing?"
- If they refuse to specify, the "vision" is rhetoric.

### Pattern 5: "我们期待看到你更多的leadership和scope" / "We want to see more leadership and scope from you"

- **Real intent**: Permanently defer promotion/raise. "Leadership" and "scope" are undefined, so unreachable.
- **Key tests**: What specific actions count as leadership? What scope expansion would qualify for the next level?
- **Recommended response**: "Manager, thanks for the feedback. To pursue this efficiently: (1) which specific behaviors count as leadership? Please give 3-5 examples. (2) What measurable scope expansion would qualify for promotion? (3) If I meet these, would I be promoted next cycle?"

### Pattern 6: "你现在的资历还不够" / "You don't have enough seniority yet"

- **Real intent**: Use intangible "seniority" to block deserved promotion.
- **Key tests**: Among peers who were promoted, what was their "seniority" at that point? Can the gap with you be specified?
- **Recommended response**: "I've noticed [specific peer] was promoted at this point. Could you specify the capability gap between us, and how long until I close it?"

### Pattern 7: "等到下一个review周期再讨论" / "Let's discuss in the next review cycle"

- **Real intent**: Push current decision into vague future.
- **Recommended response**: "Manager, between now and the next cycle, what specific work would tilt the discussion in my favor?"

## Category 3: 内疚操控话术 / Guilt Manipulation Rhetoric

Core mechanism: make the worker feel guilty for normal professional requests.

### Pattern 8: "你这样会让团队失望" / "This would let the team down"

- **Real intent**: Use collective pressure to extract individual sacrifice.
- **Deeper logic**: "Team" and "company" are deliberately conflated. Refusing overtime affects the company, but rhetoric reframes it as failing your peers.
- **Recommended response**: "What's letting the team down is understaffing, unrealistic project plans, refusal to pay for overtime. As a team member, I've fulfilled my contractual responsibilities. If the team genuinely needs more support, the company should hire or pay overtime, not extract more unpaid work from existing employees."

### Pattern 9: "年轻人就是要多奋斗" / "Young people should hustle more"

- **Real intent**: Use age as leverage to extract harsher conditions from younger employees who lack negotiation experience.
- **Recommended response**: "I agree youth is the best time to invest. I want my investment to translate to actual growth，learning, advancement, fair compensation，not just hours. Companies that genuinely help young people grow invest in training, mentorship, and clear career paths, not overtime."

### Pattern 10: "有多少人想要你这个机会" / "Many people would want your job"

- **Real intent**: Use labor-market threat to extract acceptance of unfair conditions.
- **Deeper logic**: Marx's "reserve army of labor"，capitalists use unemployment as leverage to suppress in-job demands.
- **Recommended response**: "If many people want this role, the company can replace me easily. But you haven't, which means I provide specific value. That value should be fairly compensated. If you believe I'm immediately replaceable, please proceed with replacement."

## Category 4: 责任转移话术 / Responsibility Shifting Rhetoric

Core mechanism: convert structural problems into personal failures.

### Pattern 11: "你需要更主动一点" / "You need to be more proactive"

- **Real intent**: Reframe lack of process clarity, resources, or management as your "lack of initiative."
- **Key tests**: What specific instance shows lack of initiative? In that scenario, what resources/info/authority did you need? Did the company provide them?
- **Recommended response**: "I want to be proactive. Could you give one specific recent example? Then we can examine what support was missing for me to be proactive there."

### Pattern 12: "这是你的成长机会" / "This is a growth opportunity"

- **Real intent**: Reframe what the company should pay for as a "favor" they're doing you.
- **Key test**: If this work were done by an external contractor, what would the company pay?
- **Recommended response**: "I appreciate the opportunity. If this work has market value of $X, can we reflect that in either my compensation or a clear next-promotion commitment?"

### Pattern 13: "你不能只关心自己的scope" / "Don't just focus on your own scope"

- **Real intent**: Get scope-out work without scope-out compensation.
- **Recommended response**: "Agreed I should see the bigger picture. Specifically, which out-of-scope responsibilities should I take on? What corresponding compensation? If temporary, what's the end date? If permanent, can we formally expand my scope and pay?"

## Category 5: 紧迫感操控话术 / Urgency Manipulation Rhetoric

Core mechanism: deny the worker time for clear-headed decision-making.

### Pattern 14: "我们需要你今晚就给答复" / "We need your answer tonight"

- **Real intent**: Push decision before worker can think clearly. Common in offer negotiations, performance discussions, overtime requests.
- **Reality check**: Almost no important decision genuinely requires same-day response.
- **Recommended response**: "This is an important decision. I'd like to consider it carefully. I'll respond within 24-48 hours."
- If they say "then forget it"，you've been spared a manipulative employer.

### Pattern 15: "现在不接受这个offer就没有了" / "If you don't accept now, the offer disappears"

- **Real intent**: Force decision via fake urgency.
- **Reality**: Real employers give reasonable consideration time. "Vanishing now" threat means either (a) they don't really want you, or (b) they're hiding offer flaws behind urgency.
- **Recommended response**: "I understand timing pressure. I want to consider seriously. I can commit to responding in X days. If that doesn't work, I respect your decision."

## Category 6: AI时代的新型话术 / New Rhetoric in the AI Era

These emerged after 2024, but their structure is identical to older manipulation.

### Pattern 16: "我们需要你写更详细的文档帮助team" / "We need you to write more detailed documentation for the team"

- **Real intent**: Get the user to write down all their know-how so the company can train AI to replace them.
- **Deeper logic**: This is the Colleague Skill / AI distillation pattern. See Koki Xu's anti-distillation skill (April 2026).
- **Recommended response**: "I support knowledge management. I want clarity on: (1) Who owns these documents? Can I use my own content after leaving? (2) Will these docs train AI replacing related roles? If yes, I want corresponding 'automation severance' protections."
- Also consider preserving "non-formalizable" elements in the docs (judgment, context-dependent decisions) per the anti-distillation strategy.

### Pattern 17: "你必须更AI fluent，不然会被替代" / "You must become more AI-fluent or be replaced"

- **Real intent**: Convert structural AI-era problem into individual learning failure.
- **Deeper logic**: This rhetoric makes employees self-blame ("I didn't learn enough") rather than question the system ("the company captures all the AI productivity gains").
- **Recommended response**: "I'll continue learning AI. Meanwhile, I want to discuss: as AI raises company productivity, how does the savings flow back to employees? Higher pay? Shorter hours? Profit-sharing? If AI doubles company revenue but employee compensation doesn't change, that's a distribution issue, not a technology issue."

### Pattern 18: "我们公司是AI-forward的，每个人都在用AI" / "We're AI-forward; everyone uses AI"

- **Real intent**: Use peer pressure to make employees expose all their workflows to AI without protection.
- **Deeper logic**: This is the tokenmaxxing phenomenon. Employees compete on token consumption rather than real output, leading to performative AI use.
- **Recommended response**: "I support adopting AI. The way I use AI is to optimize my real output quality, not to perform for management. The company should evaluate results, not token consumption or AI-usage frequency."

## Detection Methodology

When analyzing user-provided workplace text:

1. **Sentence-by-sentence scan**: Read each sentence and check against all 18 patterns.
2. **Multiple matches are common**: Real workplace messages often combine 2-4 patterns.
3. **Note pattern combinations**: "Family rhetoric + guilt manipulation" together is harder to resist than either alone.
4. **Check for what's NOT said**: Sometimes the manipulation is in the absence (e.g., evaluation without specific examples; promotion mention without timeline).
5. **Calibrate severity**: A single pattern instance is mild. Multiple patterns + power imbalance + emotional pressure = serious red flag.

## What's NOT Manipulation

Be intellectually honest. The following are usually fine:

- Specific factual feedback ("In the March 15 review, you missed the design review meeting")
- Clear commitments ("If you ship Project X by July 1, you're promoted in Q3")
- Direct requests with reasoning ("We need weekend work this once because [specific reason], with [specific compensation]")
- Honest negative feedback when warranted
- Rejecting a request you made (companies have the right to say no too)

If the user's shared text is mostly this kind of communication, tell them so. Crying wolf undermines the tool's credibility.
