# United Kingdom Employment Law Map

> **Last verified**: 2026-04-29
>
> Not legal advice. UK rights depend on worker/employee status, length of service, nation, contract, union coverage, and tribunal deadlines.

## Core Framework

- Employment Rights Act 1996
- Equality Act 2010
- Working Time Regulations 1998
- ACAS Early Conciliation and Employment Tribunal process

## Key Rules

### Working time

- Working Time Regulations limit average weekly working time to 48 hours, usually averaged over 17 weeks, unless the worker validly opts out.
- Workers generally have 11 hours daily rest, weekly rest, rest breaks where the working day is over 6 hours, and paid annual leave.
- Overtime premium is not generally required by statute; it depends on contract, but total pay must comply with minimum wage rules.

### Unfair dismissal

- Current ordinary unfair-dismissal qualifying period is generally 2 years for employees who started on or after 2012-04-06.
- Some dismissals are automatically unfair and do not require the same qualifying period, including many whistleblowing, health and safety, pregnancy/maternity, family leave, trade union and working-time rights scenarios.
- Tribunal time limits are short. Many claims require starting ACAS Early Conciliation within roughly 3 months less 1 day of the act complained of.
- Government plans indicate ordinary unfair-dismissal qualifying periods may change from 2027; verify current GOV.UK guidance before advising on future dates.

### Redundancy

- Redundancy should involve consultation, fair selection, alternatives/redeployment where appropriate, notice, and statutory redundancy pay if eligible.

### PIP / Capability

For capability dismissal, the employer generally needs fair reason and fair procedure: clear concerns, evidence, support, reasonable time to improve, chance to respond, and appeal.

## Practical Reality

ACAS is a critical first stop. The user should not miss tribunal deadlines while negotiating internally.
