# Australia Employment Law Map

> **Last verified**: 2026-04-29
>
> Not legal advice. Australian rights depend on employee status, award/enterprise agreement, high-income threshold, employer size, and strict filing deadlines.

## Core Framework

- Fair Work Act 2009
- National Employment Standards (NES)
- Modern Awards and enterprise agreements
- Fair Work Ombudsman (information/enforcement) and Fair Work Commission (tribunal)
- State/territory anti-discrimination and work health/safety laws

## Key Rules

### Hours and overtime

- NES baseline full-time hours are 38 hours/week plus reasonable additional hours.
- Whether additional hours are reasonable depends on role, health and safety, compensation, notice, family responsibilities, business needs and other factors.
- Overtime/penalty rates usually come from a modern award or enterprise agreement. Award-free/high-income employees may not have the same overtime entitlements.

### Unfair dismissal

- Fair Work guidance: unfair dismissal is harsh, unjust or unreasonable; not genuine redundancy; and, for small businesses, not consistent with the Small Business Fair Dismissal Code.
- Application deadline is short: generally 21 days from dismissal.
- Minimum employment period: usually 6 months, or 12 months for a small business employer.
- Eligibility can depend on award/agreement coverage or income threshold.

### Redundancy

- Must be a genuine redundancy: job no longer required, consultation obligations met, and redeployment considered where required.
- Redundancy pay depends on length of service and exceptions.

### PIP

A failed PIP is not automatically a valid reason. The employer still needs a valid reason and procedural fairness: warning, chance to respond, support, and proportionate decision.

## Practical Reality

Australia has stronger process protections than the US, but deadlines are unforgiving. If dismissed, act within days, not weeks.

## Lawyer / Official Help Triggers

- Dismissal, redundancy, forced resignation, bullying, discrimination, adverse action.
- Any Fair Work deadline approaching.
- Award classification, underpayment, overtime/penalty-rate dispute.
- Settlement deed, non-compete/restraint, visa sponsorship issue.
