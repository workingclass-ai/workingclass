# 香港 / 台湾劳动法地图 / Hong Kong & Taiwan Labour Law Map

> **Last verified**: 2026-04-29
>
> 不是法律建议。香港和台湾是两个不同法域，不要混用规则。

## Hong Kong

### Core Framework

- Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57)
- Minimum Wage Ordinance (Cap. 608)
- Mandatory Provident Fund Schemes Ordinance
- Sex Discrimination Ordinance, Disability Discrimination Ordinance, Family Status Discrimination Ordinance, Race Discrimination Ordinance

### Key Rules

- **Minimum wage**: Hong Kong has a broad Statutory Minimum Wage. It is not limited to only some industries. Exceptions include categories such as live-in domestic workers, specified student interns/work-experience students, and people outside Employment Ordinance coverage.
- **Current SMW timing**: HK$42.1/hour applies from 2025-05-01 through 2026-04-30; HK$43.1/hour applies from 2026-05-01.
- **Continuous contract**: Since 2026-01-18, the old "418" rule changed. A continuous contract generally requires four weeks' continuous employment plus either at least 17 hours each week, or 68+ hours over the relevant four-week period if one week falls below 17 hours.
- **Overtime**: No general statutory overtime premium. Overtime pay depends mainly on contract, wage structure, and whether minimum wage is still satisfied.
- **Termination**: Often notice/pay in lieu driven. Some protections and remedies depend on continuous contract status and reason for dismissal.
- **Long service payment / severance payment**: Eligibility is technical; ask the Labour Department or a Hong Kong employment lawyer before signing.

### Practical Reality

Hong Kong offers weaker maximum-hours and overtime-premium protection than Mainland China or Taiwan, but minimum wage, anti-discrimination, MPF, notice, statutory leave, and continuous-contract protections still matter.

## Taiwan

### Core Framework

- 《劳动基准法》 / Labor Standards Act
- 《性别平等工作法》
- 《就业服务法》
- 劳工退休金制度

### Key Rules

- **Working time**: Regular working time may not exceed 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week. Do not use the old "two weeks 84 hours" framing for current baseline analysis.
- **Overtime**: Overtime premiums vary by weekday/rest day/holiday and hours worked. For a specific case, check the latest Ministry of Labor calculator/guidance.
- **Termination**: Distinguish ordinary termination, layoff/redundancy, disciplinary dismissal, fixed-term expiry, and resignation. Severance depends on labor pension regime and facts.
- **Rest day / regular leave**: Taiwan's "one fixed day off and one rest day" rules are technical; verify current MOL guidance before quoting formulas.
- **Retirement pension**: Employer contribution is commonly 6% under the Labor Pension Act for covered employees.

### Practical Reality

Taiwan has stronger working-time and overtime rules than Hong Kong, but classification, managerial status, working-hour records, and company pressure around resignation can still drive outcomes.
