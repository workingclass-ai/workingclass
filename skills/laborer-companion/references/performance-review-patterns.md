# 绩效Review清醒解读 / Performance Review Decoder

This is the reference for the `/review` command. Performance reviews are the most carefully designed management tool. They appear "objective evaluation" but are usually pre-determined company decisions wrapped in evaluation language.

---

## What Performance Reviews Really Do

Reviews are not primarily about helping employees grow. Their actual functions are:

1. **Legal protection**: Create written documentation justifying future layoffs, demotions, denied promotions.
2. **Budget management**: Distribute predetermined raise/promotion budget among employees.
3. **Behavior shaping**: Use "expectations" and "criticism" to steer employees toward company's desired direction.

Note: none of these is "objectively reflect actual performance."

Understanding this is the first step.

---

## Four Language Types in Reviews

Classify each section of a review into one of these categories:

### Type A: 评价类语言 / Evaluation Language

- **Form**: What you did and how well
- **Examples**: "You completed Project X this quarter," "You helped the team solve Y"
- **Truthfulness**: Usually factually true. But pay attention to what's emphasized vs. what's minimized.
- **Warning signs**:
  - Major contributions described in vague language
  - Specific achievements credited to "team" while small problems pinned on you individually
  - Achievements described in vague terms while problems described in specific terms

### Type B: 期待类语言 / Expectation Language

- **Form**: What they want next
- **Examples**: "We want to see more leadership," "Hope to see you reach the next level next quarter"
- **Truthfulness**: **Almost always rhetoric**.
- **Why**: Genuinely supportive managers give specific, quantifiable, time-bound expectations. Vague "expectations" almost always serve to:
  - Set up justification for not promoting you
  - Continue extracting effort with no reward
  - Create written record for future termination
- **Key tests**:
  - "More leadership" → Which specific behaviors? Defined by whom? Evaluated how?
  - "Reach the next level" → Which specific level? On what timeline?
  - "More proactive" → Which specific moment did you fail to be proactive?

### Type C: 批评类语言 / Criticism Language

- **Form**: What you did wrong
- **Two subtypes**:

**C1: Specific criticism (legitimate)**
- Has specific scenario, specific behavior, specific consequence
- Example: "On March 15, you didn't notify design team about the meeting time, which delayed feedback by a week"
- You can accept, refute, or improve

**C2: Vague criticism (usually rhetoric)**
- No specific examples, no specific scenarios, no specific consequences
- Example: "Your communication style needs adjustment"
- Cannot be refuted, cannot be improved，designed to create written "you have a problem" record

- **Warning signs**:
  - All criticism is vague (no specific examples)
  - When you ask for examples, response is "it's not about one thing, it's overall feeling"
  - Things you've never been told before suddenly appear in review

### Type D: 未来奖励类语言 / Future Reward Language

- **Form**: "If you do X, you'll get Y in the future"
- **Examples**: "If you continue performing, we can consider promotion next review," "I'll fully recommend you," "You'll be rewarded long-term"
- **Truthfulness**: **99% rhetoric**.
- **Why**:
  - Real future rewards have written backing (in email, contract, salary agreements)
  - Verbal promises have no legal/institutional protection
  - Managers leave, CEOs leave, policies change，all verbal promises evaporate
  - Even if manager stays, they can say "circumstances changed"
- **Key tests**:
  - Can you write this commitment down?
  - Can you get email confirmation?
  - If they say "I personally guarantee"，ask "What if you change jobs?"

## Five Common Review Traps

For each trap: signal, intent, identification, response.

### Trap 1: Unreachable High Expectations

- **Form**: Review says you're doing OK, but sets vague/unreachable "next steps"
- **Intent**: Block promotion/raise for the next year. "You haven't met our expectations yet."
- **Identification**: Expectations have no quantifiable metrics, no timeline, no concrete actions
- **Response**: Demand SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). If they refuse to specify, the "expectation" exists to never reward you.

### Trap 2: Positive Verbal + Negative Rating

- **Form**: Verbal feedback is positive but rating/score is mediocre
- **Intent**: Make you feel good (hard to refute) while preparing the no-raise/no-promotion decision
- **Identification**:
  - Manager says "great work" but rating is "Meets expectations" or below
  - Manager says "we believe in you" but raise is below company average
- **Response**: "Manager, you said I'm doing great, but my rating is 'Meets.' Could you reconcile this gap?" Force them to align verbal and written. Either change the rating or admit the verbal is exaggerated.

### Trap 3: "Soft" PIP

- **Form**: Not formal PIP, but review contains lots of criticism and "improvement areas"
- **Intent**: Build groundwork for future no-promotion or termination
- **Identification**:
  - Previous reviews were positive, this one suddenly has many criticisms
  - Things being criticized you've never been told about before
  - All criticism is vague ("communication," "initiative," "team spirit")
  - Manager evades concrete discussion when you push
- **Response**:
  - Be alert immediately. This is often pre-termination paperwork.
  - Email manager: "Are these criticisms considered 'performance issues'? Will they affect my employment?"
  - Manager's answer is critical:
    - Says "no" → save email as evidence
    - Says "we'll monitor your progress" → it's a soft PIP, start planning exit
  - Update resume immediately, prepare external opportunities

### Trap 4: Scope Creep Without Compensation

- **Form**: Review acknowledges your scope and impact have grown, but no salary discussion
- **Intent**: Get more work without paying more
- **Identification**:
  - Your workload visibly increased over past year
  - You took on tasks outside your original role
  - Review acknowledges your "contributions" and "impact"
  - Review doesn't discuss raise or promotion
- **Response**:
  - "I've noticed my scope expanded to include X, Y, Z. Can we discuss salary adjustment for the expanded scope?"
  - If "not now," ask "When? What conditions?"
  - Document the conversation
  - If 6 months pass without delivery, start external job search. Use this expanded scope as evidence for new salary negotiation

### Trap 5: Individual Contribution "Team-Washed"

- **Form**: You specifically led something major, but review describes it as "team accomplishment"
- **Intent**: When bonuses/equity/promotions distributed, company can say "it's a team effort, everyone contributed"，reducing your individual reward
- **Identification**:
  - You privately know something major was led by you
  - Review describes it as team accomplishment
  - Same "team" language used in colleagues' reviews
- **Response**:
  - Demand individual contribution recorded: "I agree it's a team accomplishment. I'd also like specific record of my individual contributions: X, Y, Z. This matters for my career development."
  - If manager refuses, this is a warning signal that they don't intend to credit your individual work

## Decoding Workflow

### Step 1: Read the entire review

Tag each section as Evaluation/Expectation/Criticism/Future Reward.

### Step 2: Test each section's specificity

- Evaluation: specific events or vague "good"?
- Expectation: quantifiable with timeline?
- Criticism: specific scenarios or abstract accusations?
- Future Reward: writable, time-bound, verifiable?

### Step 3: Find "discrepancy points"

Compare different parts of the review:
- Verbal feedback vs. written rating，gap?
- Evaluation vs. reward，gap (high evaluation but low raise)?
- Evaluation vs. expectation，gap (good evaluation but harsh expectation)?

Discrepancy points are where rhetoric hides.

### Step 4: Determine real intent

Based on analysis, identify the most likely real purpose:

- ✅ **Genuine support**: Wants to develop and reward you (rare but real)
- ⚠️ **Keep but underpay**: Wants retention without more rewards (most common)
- 🚩 **Skip this promotion**: Setting up justification
- 🚩🚩 **Soft PIP / pre-firing**: Building written evidence
- 🚩🚩🚩 **Constructive dismissal**: Wants you to quit voluntarily

### Step 5: Calibrated response

Different intents → different responses:

| Intent | Response Pattern |
|--------|------------------|
| Genuine support | Ask for specifics to maximize growth |
| Keep but underpay | Demand specifics, set up clear future negotiation |
| Skip promotion | Document carefully, demand written promotion criteria |
| Soft PIP | Start parallel job search, document everything |
| Constructive dismissal | Don't quit voluntarily，make them fire you with severance |

## Two Detailed Examples

### Example 1: Vague Future Reward

**Review text**:
> "Your work this quarter is overall not bad. We saw you complete Project A and contribute to the team. Looking forward to seeing more leadership and scope from you next quarter. We're very optimistic about your long-term development，as long as you keep growing, many opportunities await."

**Decoding**:

1. "Overall not bad" = vague evaluation, doesn't say what's good
2. "Complete Project A" = only specific recognition, but you privately know you did much more
3. "Contribute to team" = specific contribution unspecified
4. "More leadership and scope" = vague expectation, no metrics
5. "Optimistic about long-term" = future reward rhetoric
6. "Keep growing, many opportunities" = pure rhetoric, no specificity

**Real meaning**: "We're not promoting you this round, but want you to keep working hard."

**Recommended response email**:

> "Manager, thanks for the review. To efficiently meet your 'leadership and scope' expectations, could you help me clarify:
>
> 1. Which specific behaviors count as leadership? Please give 3-5 examples.
> 2. What measurable scope expansion would qualify for promotion? Please give quantitative metrics.
> 3. If I meet these criteria, would I be promoted next review?
>
> I want our goals aligned so I can invest efficiently."

**Outcome interpretation**:
- If manager replies with specifics → save email as future review evidence
- If manager evades or gives more vague language → real intent confirmed: you won't be promoted next time either. Plan external opportunities.

### Example 2: Soft PIP Signal

**Review text**:
> "You've had some communication issues recently. Some team members feedback that you're not being proactive enough. Recommend you improve."

**Decoding**:

Warning signs:
1. Vague criticism ("communication issues," "not proactive")
2. Anonymous source ("some team members")
3. No specific examples
4. Sudden appearance (not in previous reviews)

**Real meaning** (most likely → least likely):
1. Manager building groundwork for no-promotion
2. Manager building evidence for potential termination
3. Manager personally has issue with you, packaged as "team feedback"

**Recommended response email**:

> "Thanks for the feedback. To improve, could you provide:
>
> 1. Which specific scenarios showed communication problems? Please give 3 recent examples.
> 2. Who in the team gave this feedback? Or at least which specific events did they reference?
> 3. Are these considered performance issues? Will they affect my employment or next review?"

**Outcome interpretation**:
- If they give specific examples and say "not a performance issue" → save conversation, improve specific behaviors
- If they evade specific examples or say "yes, will affect" → it's a soft PIP. Immediately:
  1. Update resume
  2. Contact external opportunities
  3. Prepare severance negotiation
  4. Don't voluntarily resign (let company pay the cost of you leaving)

## Important Reminders

1. **Not all reviews are rhetoric**. Some managers are genuine. If a review has high specificity, quantifiable metrics, timelines, and accountability, that's a good manager，appreciate them.

2. **Save written evidence**. All important conversations via email/chat. These become priceless later.

3. **Act within 48 hours**. After a problematic review, don't delay. Delay only helps the company.

4. **Reviews are business negotiations, not moral judgments**. Don't emotionalize. Treat as commercial dialogue.

5. **You're not isolated**. If you spot systemic patterns (company uses similar rhetoric on everyone), discuss with 1-2 trusted colleagues. Collective clarity is more powerful than individual clarity.

---

The biggest harm of a performance review isn't unfair evaluation.

It's making you believe the evaluation is fair, leading you to accept arrangements you shouldn't.

Decoding it clearly is one of the most important things you can do for yourself.
